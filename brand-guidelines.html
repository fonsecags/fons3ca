06   Voice

Tone of Voice

First person, experience-led, no hedging. The writing sounds like someone who has done the work and is sharing what they actually learned, not performing expertise.

Write like this

  • Direct. Short sentences. No padding.
  • First person. "I have seen", "I run accounts", "I learned".
  • Concrete over abstract. Data, examples, specifics.
  • Humble but confident. State opinions as opinions.
  • Contrarian when warranted. Call out received wisdom.
  • Story-forward. The context is as important as the point.

Not like this

  • Buzzwords or agency-speak. "Leverage synergies".
  • Hedging. "It could be argued that perhaps..."
  • Sales copy. Calls to action that feel like ads.
  • Passive voice. "Results were delivered by the team".
  • Listicles with no narrative thread.
  • Em dashes. Never. Use a comma or a full stop.
