There is a version of AI in marketing that is genuinely exciting. Campaigns that learn faster, creative that adapts in real time, bidding strategies that respond to signals no human could process. I have seen it work. When the conditions are right, it is remarkable.

The conditions are almost never right.

## What AI actually runs on

Smart Bidding, responsive ads, Performance Max, Demand Gen. Every AI-powered system in Google Ads is making decisions based on inputs. Conversion data, audience signals, creative assets, bid targets. The AI does not have opinions. It has patterns. And the patterns it finds are only as good as the data you feed it.

I have audited dozens of accounts where teams have handed control to AI-powered campaigns and watched performance collapse. In almost every case, the problem was not the AI. It was the brief.

Conversion tracking firing on the wrong event. A target ROAS set based on what the team wanted rather than what the data supported. Asset groups with one image and three headlines, all saying the same thing. Audience signals pulled from a broad remarketing list with no real intent behind it.

Feed that into any learning algorithm and the algorithm will optimise it. Just not in the direction you wanted.

## The brief has not gone away

When I talk about a brief in this context, I mean everything that tells the campaign what success looks like before it launches. The conversion action. The target. The creative. The audience signals. The landing page.

Every one of those is a decision. Every one of them shapes what the AI learns.

Most teams spend hours debating bid strategies and almost no time on the inputs that determine whether those strategies can work. I understand why. The inputs feel like setup. The bidding feels like the campaign. But the setup is the campaign. The AI just executes it.

## What good inputs actually look like

A clean conversion action that fires when a real customer takes a real action. Not a page view, not a button click that does not correlate with revenue. A purchase, a lead form submission, a phone call from someone who bought.

A target ROAS that reflects your actual margin and your actual conversion rate, not the number your client asked for. If your account converts at 3x and you set a 6x target, the campaign will not deliver 6x. It will restrict spend until it starves itself.

Creative that covers the full range of what you are trying to say, not three variations of the same headline. Different messages for different stages of intent. Different formats for different placements. Give the algorithm something to test.

Audience signals that reflect real customers. Your buyer list. High-value purchasers. People who have completed the action you care about. Not your entire site traffic.

## The honest version of the AI story

AI-powered campaigns are the best campaigns I have ever managed when the inputs are right. They are also the fastest way to burn through a budget when they are not.

The marketers winning with AI right now are not the ones who trusted it most. They are the ones who invested in the quality of what they gave it, stayed close enough to catch problems early, and understood that the algorithm is a tool, not a strategist.

The brief still matters. It always did. It just matters more now, because the machine moves faster than you can correct it.
