YouTube Shorts just received MRC brand safety accreditation. For most people, that is a compliance headline. For performance marketers, it is a signal worth paying attention to.

Brand safety accreditation from the Media Rating Council is not just a badge. It is the thing that unlocks budget. Large advertisers, particularly those in regulated industries, cannot run on platforms that have not been independently verified for brand safety. That has kept significant spend off Shorts since it launched. That is now changing.

## Why this matters for performance

Short-form video is where attention is. The data on time spent has been clear for several years. What has been less clear is whether that attention could be converted into performance outcomes at a cost that made sense.

The early Shorts numbers are more interesting than the sceptics expected. The format's brevity forces a kind of creative discipline that longer formats do not. You have six seconds to make someone care. That constraint produces better creative more often than you might think.

The audiences on Shorts also skew younger than traditional YouTube, and in many categories younger audiences are exactly where the pipeline problem is. They are not unreachable. They are just unreachable through the channels most performance teams are comfortable with.

## What I have tested

I have been running Shorts placements as part of broader YouTube campaigns for clients in e-commerce for the past year. What I have found:

The CPMs are lower than long-form. Significantly lower in most categories. The click-through rates are also lower, which some teams use as a reason to dismiss the format. That is the wrong read. The conversion journey from short-form video is different, not absent. Attribution needs to account for view-through and assisted conversions, not just direct clicks.

The creative that works on Shorts is not the same creative that works on long-form or on social. The hook has to be immediate. The message has to be single. The call to action has to be obvious. Teams that repurpose existing video assets and wonder why performance is poor are making a category error.

## The practical implication

MRC accreditation does not mean Shorts is right for every brand or every campaign objective. What it means is that the conversation can now be had without brand safety being the reason it stops.

If you have been excluding Shorts because of brand safety concerns, those concerns are now addressed. What you do with that is a strategic question.

My view: test it with a modest budget, build creative that is actually native to the format, and measure it with an attribution model that reflects how short-form video actually works in a customer journey. Do not expect it to perform like Search. Expect it to perform like top-of-funnel video, because that is what it is.

The channel is real. The audience is there. The question is whether your team is willing to learn a new format before your competitors do.
