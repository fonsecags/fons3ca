Every week I speak to a marketing team that is either fighting Performance Max or blindly trusting it. Both approaches miss the point.

PMax is not a campaign type. It is a signal-collection engine that also happens to serve ads.

## The mistake most teams make

The typical response to PMax underperformance is to pull back. Add negative keywords, tighten audience signals, reduce the budget. Sometimes that is the right call. But before you do any of that, you should spend time reading what the campaign is actually telling you.

Because buried in the asset group reports, the search category insights, and the audience data is a detailed picture of how Google's AI understands your business and your customers. That picture is often more honest than the one your team has been working from.

## The three places the signal lives

**Asset performance labels.** When Google marks a headline as "Low" while your team's favourite copy sits at "Best," that is data. Not opinion, data. I have seen accounts where the assets performing best were the ones the creative team liked least. The market does not share our aesthetic preferences.

**Search categories.** PMax does not show you search terms the way Search campaigns do, but it does show you category-level intent. If you are seeing volume in categories you did not intend to target, that tells you something real. Either your creative is attracting the wrong audience, or the right audience is describing their problem differently than you assumed.

**Audience insights.** The segments that over-index in your PMax audiences often reveal customer profiles you are not actively marketing to. Some of those will be noise. Others will be your next growth opportunity.

## What I do with this

I treat the first 60-90 days of a PMax campaign as a research phase as much as a performance phase. I am not just optimising ROAS. I am learning how Google interprets the business, where it finds traction, and what signals are driving results.

That information then feeds back into the whole account. Better briefs for creative. More precise audience targeting in Search and Shopping. A sharper understanding of which customer segments actually convert.

The teams getting the most from PMax are not the ones who gave it the most control. They are the ones who stayed close enough to understand what it was learning.

## The bigger point

AI-powered campaigns do not replace strategic thinking. They generate more data, faster, about what is working. The question is whether your team is set up to read that data and act on it.

Most are not. They are optimising the campaign instead of learning from it.

That is the gap. And it is a wide one.
